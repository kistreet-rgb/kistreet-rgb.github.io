What is Mechatronics?

Mechatronics is the multidisciplinary intersection of mechanical engineering, electrical engineering, and computer science. Rather than treating these disciplines in isolation, mechatronics focuses on combining them to create smart, automated, and highly efficient systems.

As a mechatronics engineer, I am trained to view a system holistically. Whether working in a purely mechanical role designing structural components, an electrical role routing a PCB, or seamlessly integrating both with software logic, I have the versatile toolkit required to adapt to complex engineering challenges.

Why I Chose Mechatronics

My path into engineering started with a natural curiosity for how physical systems fit together. From building PCs and intricate Lego sets to the hands-on process of maintaining and tuning vehicles, I have always loved assembling individual components into high-performance machines.

I chose mechatronics because it allows me to scale that passion. It provides the formal engineering principles to not just assemble, but to design, automate, and innovate from scratch. I didn't want to be boxed into strictly software or hardware; I wanted the technical foundation to bridge the physical and digital worlds seamlessly.
